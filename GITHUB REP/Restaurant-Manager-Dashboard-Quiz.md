### Core System Operations

**Q51. Explain the complete process of adding a new Chef Special from start to finish.**

**Sample Answer:** To add a Chef Special, navigate to the Chef Specials page and click the "Add" button. This opens a modal where you enter the dish name (e.g., "Grilled Salmon"), description highlighting key ingredients and preparation, the price, and select the appropriate category (Appetizer, Main Course, Dessert, etc.). Upload an appetizing photo of the dish to help guests visualize it. Ensure the "Active" status is selected so guests can immediately view and order it. Click "Save" to add the special to your menu. The new special will now appear on guest Luxegenie devices.

---

**Q52. Walk through the complete workflow of seating a reservation guest who just arrived.**

**Sample Answer:** First, navigate to the Reservations page and use the "Today" filter to find the guest's reservation by their name or time. Verify the party size matches the reservation details. Navigate to the Tables view and identify an appropriate vacant table that accommodates their party size. Click on the table to open the reservation modal, then select "Allot Table" and choose the correct reservation from the list. Guide the guests to their physical table and seat them. Finally, go to the Luxegenies page, select an available device with good battery, and assign it to the newly allotted table so guests can begin ordering.

---

**Q53. Describe how to handle table merging when a large party of 10 arrives but your biggest table seats only 6.**

**Sample Answer:** In the Tables view, enable the table selection mode by clicking the merge/select option. Select two or more adjacent tables whose combined capacity will accommodate the party of 10 (for example, one 6-seater and one 4-seater). Once the appropriate tables are selected, click the "Merge Tables" button to link them together. The system will treat these as one combined table for the session. Seat the party across the merged tables and assign a Luxegenie device to the merged table group. When the party leaves and you process their bill, use the "Unmerge" option to separate the tables back into individual units for future use.

---

### Device & Technology Management

**Q54. Explain what to do if a guest's Luxegenie device freezes or becomes unresponsive during their meal.**

**Sample Answer:** First, apologize to the guest and immediately take any pending orders manually to ensure service continuity. Navigate to the Luxegenies page and identify the problematic device by its assigned table number. Try using the "Reboot" function to restart the device remotely - this often resolves software freezes. While waiting for the reboot, select another available Luxegenie device with full battery and assign it to the table. Unassign the frozen device and give the replacement device to the guest, ensuring their session information transfers correctly. After service, investigate the frozen device further - perform a hard reset if needed, check for software updates, or report persistent issues for technical support.

---

**Q55. Describe the complete lifecycle management of a Luxegenie device during a typical dining session.**

**Sample Answer:** When guests are seated, navigate to the Luxegenies page and select an available device with adequate battery charge. Use the "Assign to Table" function to link the device to the specific table number where guests are seated. Physically deliver the device to the table so guests can browse the menu and place orders throughout their meal. The device remains assigned during the entire dining experience, tracking all orders and service requests. When guests request the bill, process their payment through the system. After guests leave, immediately go back to the Luxegenies page and use the "Unassign" function to disconnect the device from that table, making it available for the next guests. Clean the device and place it on a charging station if the battery is below 50%.

### Problem Solving & Decision Making

**Q62. A reservation for 6 people arrives, but two additional guests joined them. How do you handle this using the dashboard?**

**Sample Answer:** First, check the Tables view to see if the currently allotted table can physically accommodate 8 people - some 6-seater tables have extra space. If not, look for a vacant 8-person table nearby and use the "Allot Table" function to reassign the reservation to the larger table. If no single large table is available, check if you can merge an adjacent smaller table with their current table to create adequate space. Update the reservation details in the system to reflect 8 guests instead of 6 for accurate record-keeping. Communicate with kitchen and service staff about the party size increase. If absolutely no solution exists, honestly explain the situation and ask if some guests can wait at the bar while you prepare appropriate seating.

**Q63. The system shows three tables as "Occupied" but you can see they're actually empty and cleaned. What should you do?**

**Sample Answer:** This situation indicates incomplete session closures from the previous guests. First, physically verify each table is truly empty, cleared, and ready for new guests. Navigate to the Manage Sessions page and check each table's session details to see when they started and confirm no active orders exist. If sessions were never properly closed, end each session manually using the system controls. Visit the Luxegenies page and unassign any devices still linked to these tables. Finally, manually change each table's status back to "Vacant" in the Tables view. This incident highlights the need to coach staff on completing entire checkout workflows - processing payment, ending session, unassigning device, and confirming vacant status. Consider implementing a checklist for closing procedures to prevent recurrence.

---

**Q64. A guest calls to modify their reservation time from 7 PM to 8 PM. Walk through how you'd handle this request.**

**Sample Answer:** Navigate to the Reservations page and use the search function to find the guest's reservation by name or phone number. Check if their party size remains the same or if they need to modify that too. Before saving, look at the 8 PM time slot in the reservations list to ensure you're not creating conflicts with existing bookings and that you'll have appropriate table availability. Save the updated reservation. Immediately call the guest back to confirm the change and provide a confirmation number if your system generates one. Consider sending a text reminder for the new time to prevent confusion on the reservation date.

**Q65. Multiple Recent Activities show service requests from several tables. How do you prioritize and respond?**

### System Understanding

**Q68. Explain the relationship between Reservations, Tables, Sessions, and Luxegenies in the system workflow.**

**Sample Answer:** These four components work together in a connected workflow. A Reservation is a future booking containing guest information and desired dining time. When guests arrive, the reservation is linked to a physical Table through the "Allot Table" function, changing the table status to "Alloted" then "Occupied." This creates a Session - the active dining period tracking all orders, timings, and interactions for that table. A Luxegenie device is assigned to the table/session, enabling guests to interact with the menu and request services. All four elements must be properly coordinated - the reservation provides guest data, the table provides physical location, the session tracks the dining experience, and the Luxegenie enables digital interaction. When guests leave, the session ends, the device is unassigned, and the table returns to vacant status.

**Q69. Why is it important to keep accurate table statuses in real-time, and what problems arise from inaccuracies?**

**Sample Answer:** Accurate real-time table status is fundamental to restaurant operations. When statuses are wrong, you might turn away walk-in guests believing you're full when tables are actually vacant, losing revenue. You could double-book tables by assigning the same table to multiple reservations or guests, creating service disasters and guest dissatisfaction. Inaccurate statuses prevent effective wait time estimates for guests, damaging trust and experience. They cause confusion among staff about which tables need service, bussing, or setup. Operational metrics become unreliable, hampering management decisions. Financial reporting can be affected if sessions aren't properly tracked. Maintaining accuracy requires training all staff to complete workflows fully - opening sessions when guests sit, closing sessions when they leave, and updating statuses throughout the dining experience.

**Q70. Explain how the manager dashboard helps coordinate between front-of-house and back-of-house operations.**

**Sample Answer:** The Manager Dashboard serves as a central communication hub linking dining room and kitchen operations. When guests place orders through Luxegenies, they immediately appear in the kitchen system, eliminating verbal miscommunication. The Recent Activities feature alerts front-of-house staff to guest requests, ensuring quick responses. Table status visibility helps kitchen staff understand current capacity and order flow patterns. The Chef Specials module ensures servers and kitchen staff are aligned on available dishes - when kitchen runs low on ingredients, the special can be deactivated immediately, preventing servers from taking orders that can't be fulfilled. Session information tracks order timing, helping kitchens prioritize preparation. Reservation data gives kitchens advance notice of party sizes and special requests for proper prep planning. This coordinated information flow reduces errors, improves timing, and creates smoother service for guests.

For the 6 walk-in parties, check their party sizes and compare with vacant tables or tables likely to become available soon. Provide realistic wait time estimates to walk-ins based on table status. If you have multiple walk-ins of similar size, create a simple queue ordering them by arrival sequence. Use the Reservations page to verify no upcoming bookings will need these tables soon. If some walk-in parties fit available tables, seat them immediately and assign Luxegenies quickly. Communicate with all staff about the high volume to ensure coordination. Monitor Recent Activities continuously to ensure no new requests are overlooked during this busy period.

**Q74. SCENARIO: A Luxegenie device crashes during a large party's meal. They've already ordered appetizers (which arrived) and were browsing for main courses. Walk through your complete response.**

Deliver the working replacement device to the table and verify that guests can see their order history. Manually input the main course orders you collected on paper into the system. After service, troubleshoot the crashed device - try hard reset, check for software updates, or report to technical support if problems persist. If that fails, use your alternative table arrangement. Assign Luxegenies to the merged tables as soon as ready. Throughout, maintain communication with the arriving party so they don't feel forgotten, and consider a complimentary appetizer to compensate for any waiting inconvenience.
